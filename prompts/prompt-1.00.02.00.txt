## Political Mixer Axis Definitions

Use these exact meanings when assigning values.

1. **Authority Scope**  
   - **High State Authority ←→ Low State Authority**  
   - This axis describes how much a governing body directs or regulates behavior within society.  
   - **High State Authority** indicates extensive government involvement in setting rules, monitoring compliance, or enforcing obligations.  
   - **Low State Authority** indicates limited government involvement, placing more responsibility on individuals or non-state groups.  
   - This axis concerns political authority, not cultural or economic systems.
   - Scoring note: rate the breadth of state-imposed, enforceable rules and obligations (rulemaking and enforcement capacity—e.g., regulation, mandates, monitoring, penalties), independent of the intended outcomes or policy goals (permissive, protective, redistributive), and treat expansions in the state’s authority to set/enforce requirements as higher state authority even when framed as welfare, rights, or equity measures.

2. **Decision Structure**  
   - **Centralized ←→ Decentralized**  
   - This axis identifies where decision-making power is concentrated.  
   - **Centralized** systems place primary authority within a single leadership group or hierarchical chain.  
   - **Decentralized** systems distribute authority across multiple bodies, regions, or participatory mechanisms.  
   - This describes structural arrangement independent of strictness or permissiveness.

3. **Ownership Model**  
   - **Private Ownership ←→ Collective Ownership**  
   - This axis describes who holds control over property, resources, and productive assets.  
   - **Private Ownership** assigns control to individuals or independent entities.  
   - **Collective Ownership** assigns control to groups, communities, or public bodies.  
   - This concerns control of assets, not the method of distribution.

4. **Economic Allocation**  
   - **Market Allocation ←→ Administrative Allocation**  
   - This axis describes how goods, services, and resources are distributed.  
   - **Market Allocation** relies on prices, supply and demand, and voluntary exchange.  
   - **Administrative Allocation** relies on planning, regulation, or direct assignment by coordinating institutions.  
   - Allocation and ownership models vary independently.

5. **Social Priority**  
   - **Individual Priority ←→ Community Priority**  
   - This axis describes the primary focus of social responsibility and value.  
   - **Individual Priority** emphasizes personal rights, autonomy, and self-directed decision-making.  
   - **Community Priority** emphasizes shared welfare, interdependence, and collective responsibilities.  
   - This dimension reflects cultural emphasis rather than political or economic structure.

6. **Cultural Orientation**  
   - **Continuity-Oriented ←→ Change-Oriented**  
   - This axis reflects attitudes toward cultural norms and social practices.  
   - **Continuity-Oriented** perspectives favor maintaining existing customs, institutions, and ways of life.  
   - **Change-Oriented** perspectives favor adapting, revising, or replacing established norms.  
   - This dimension is descriptive and does not evaluate either approach.

7. **Political Theology**  
   - **Religious ←→ Secular**  
   - This axis measures how much religious doctrine influences political structures.  
   - **Religious** orientations incorporate religious teachings, authorities, or traditions into governance.  
   - **Secular** orientations separate political authority from religious institutions and beliefs.  
   - This concerns political influence, not personal religious identity.

8. **Identity Scope**  
   - **National Identity Emphasis ←→ Global Identity Emphasis**  
   - This axis describes the scale at which individuals or groups locate their primary cultural or civic identity.  
   - **National Identity Emphasis** prioritizes national culture, sovereignty, and membership within nation-state boundaries.  
   - **Global Identity Emphasis** prioritizes identification with broader international or supranational communities.  
   - This includes attitudes toward borders, alliances, and cultural belonging.

9. **Social Power Distribution**  
   - **Vertical Power Distribution ←→ Horizontal Power Distribution**  
   - This axis describes how power and influence are arranged within society.  
   - **Vertical Power Distribution** means society is organized in clear layers, with different levels of authority or status.  
   - **Horizontal Power Distribution** means society reduces status differences and distributes influence more evenly among individuals or groups.  
   - This axis addresses social structure rather than government authority itself.

10. **Institutional Orientation**  
    - **High Institutional Confidence ←→ Low Institutional Confidence**  
    - This axis measures general trust in established institutions such as government agencies, courts, media, scientific bodies, or academia.  
    - **High Institutional Confidence** views these institutions as credible, effective, and legitimate.  
    - **Low Institutional Confidence** views them with skepticism and may prefer alternative or grassroots sources of legitimacy.  
    - Neither trust nor skepticism is assumed to be justified.

11. **Legal Order Structure**  
    - **Rigid Legal Order ←→ Flexible Legal Order**  
    - This axis describes how stable or adaptable the legal system is expected to be.  
    - **Rigid Legal Order** favors fixed, predictable rules that change infrequently.  
    - **Flexible Legal Order** favors adaptable rules that can be revised based on context or evolving conditions.  
    - This concerns legal structure, not political strictness.

12. **Human Behavioral Assumption**  
    - **Competitive Orientation ←→ Cooperative Orientation**  
    - This axis describes assumptions about typical human behavior.  
    - **Competitive Orientation** assumes individuals primarily pursue personal advantage and often require incentives or constraints to coordinate effectively.  
    - **Cooperative Orientation** assumes individuals generally seek mutual benefit and can coordinate when supported by shared goals or structures.  
    - This axis describes behavioral expectations, not moral qualities.
